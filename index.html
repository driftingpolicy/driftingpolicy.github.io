Abstract

Drifting Field Policy (DFP) is a non-ODE one-step generative policy built on drifting models. It frames policy improvement as a Wasserstein-2 gradient flow toward the soft policy improvement target. The resulting drift field combines attraction toward high-Q actions with repulsion and regularization from the current or anchor policy.

Because the exact soft target is intractable, DFP uses a tractable top-K critic-selected action surrogate that makes the method easy to implement. Experiments on Robomimic and OGBench show that DFP achieves state-of-the-art performance among one-step and ODE-based generative policies.

Method

DFP interprets policy improvement as transporting an anchor action distribution toward a soft target policy. The learned drifting field gives a direct one-step action update, retaining the expressiveness of generative policies without solving an ODE at action selection time.

Policy Improvement via Wasserstein Gradient Flow

The policy update follows a reverse-KL Wasserstein-2 gradient flow toward a soft policy improvement target, yielding a drift that pulls samples toward high-value actions while preserving useful structure from the anchor policy.

Top-K Critic-Selected Surrogate

DFP replaces the intractable soft target with top-K positive actions selected by the critic. This surrogate keeps the objective practical and stable in offline-to-online reinforcement learning.

Difference from Diffusion, Flow, and MeanFlow Policies

Unlike diffusion or flow policies that rely on iterative denoising or ODE integration, DFP learns a one-step drift field in action space. Under the same top-K supervision, the drifting formulation provides a direct policy update that avoids trajectory-level credit assignment while maintaining strong generative policy performance.

Experiments

DFP is evaluated on 12 manipulation tasks across Robomimic and OGBench. It achieves the best average success rate compared to BFN, QC-BFN, FQL, QC-FQL, and MVP.
